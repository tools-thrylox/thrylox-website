Thrylox Privacy Notice

Last updated: May 2026

What this notice covers

This notice explains how Thrylox processes information through the public website, business contact channels, and the BOG TestFlight signup flow.

Data we may process

  • Technical information such as basic server logs, browser metadata, and reliability records.
  • Contact information you voluntarily submit through business or support channels.
  • Email addresses submitted through the BOG TestFlight access form.
  • Any follow-up information you provide when discussing build access, feedback, or partner enquiries.

How we use it

  • To respond to business, compliance, partner, or vendor questions.
  • To manage access to external playtests and follow up on active BOG testing waves.
  • To connect signups with later feedback, support requests, and playtest operations.
  • To protect the website and related services from abuse, misuse, or security incidents.

Legal basis

Where applicable under EU or EEA law, we rely on legitimate interests in running a secure business, preparing or performing contractual relationships, and managing lawful product research and support operations.

Sharing and retention

  • We do not sell personal data.
  • We may use service providers for hosting, email delivery, analytics, or operations under appropriate safeguards.
  • Signup information is kept only as long as reasonably necessary to manage the relevant playtest wave, respond to follow-up requests, or satisfy legal obligations.

Your rights

Where applicable, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, or objection regarding your personal data. For privacy requests, contact admin@thrylox.com.
